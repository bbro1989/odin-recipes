Lasagna Recipe

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made by layering sheets of pasta with rich fillings such as seasoned meat, tomato sauce, creamy béchamel, and melted cheese. The layers are baked together until the top is golden and bubbly, creating a hearty, comforting meal that's both flavorful and satisfying.

Traditionally, lasagna features a ragù made with ground beef or pork, but variations include vegetarian versions with spinach, mushrooms, or zucchini. Whether served at a family dinner or a special occasion, lasagna is loved for its warm, layered texture and ability to bring people together around the table.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna pasta sheets
  • Ground beef or pork (or a mix)
  • Onion, chopped
  • Garlic, minced
  • Tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
  • Tomato paste
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • Dried oregano and basil
  • Ricotta cheese or béchamel sauce
  • Mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • Parmesan cheese, grated
  • Egg (optional, for mixing with ricotta)

Steps

  1. Prepare the meat sauce:
    Heat olive oil in a pan, sauté chopped onions and garlic, then add ground beef or pork. Cook until browned. Stir in tomato sauce, tomato paste, and herbs (like oregano and basil). Simmer for 20–30 minutes.
  2. Cook the lasagna noodles:
    Heat olive oil in a pan, sauté chopped onions and garlic, then add ground beef or pork. Cook until browned. Stir in tomato sauce, tomato paste, and herbs (like oregano and basil).
    Simmer for 20–30 minutes.
  3. Prepare the cheese mixture:
    In a bowl, mix ricotta cheese with an egg (optional), salt, pepper, and some grated Parmesan.
  4. Assemble the lasagna:
    In a bowl, mix ricotta cheese with an egg (optional), salt, pepper, and some grated Parmesan.
  5. Bake the lasagna:
    Cover the dish with foil and bake in a preheated oven at 375°F (190°C) for about 25–30 minutes.
    Remove the foil and bake for another 10–15 minutes until the top is golden and bubbly.
  6. Let it rest:
    Remove from the oven and let the lasagna sit for 10–15 minutes before cutting.
    This helps it set and makes serving easier.
